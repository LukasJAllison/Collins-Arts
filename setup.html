Art I - Intro to Art: This course gives students a basic overview of the Principles and Elements of design and how to use them to create works of art. Students are exposed to a wide array of tools and techniques as well as art media. Students must take this intro level course before moving on to more advanced art classes.

Art II - Drawing & Painting: This course gives students a more in-depth study of drawing and painting media, tools, and techniques. Artists are pushed to think more deeply about their artwork and create works of art that revolve around chosen themes. The use of Principles and Elements in artwork is emphasized more than in a typical intro level class.
Comprehensive Visual Art: Students will explore art forms more deeply than Art I, having opportunity to create with a wide range of mediums in both traditional and digital format. The use of computers to generate artwork is available, but not necessary to the completion of coursework. Instruction will focus on the development of personal artistic styles, and students will be encouraged to explore mediums. This class is an excellent precursor to AP Art.
AP Art - 2D Studio Art: AP art is a college level course that focuses on the creation of an art portfolio. 25% of the portfolio consists of selected works (the Artist's best work) and 75% consists of a sustained investigation (several pieces of art that revolve around a central theme and would be part of the same gallery or show.) A large emphasis is placed on the Elements and Principles of Design and time is devoted to pushing Artists to help further develop their personal style.
